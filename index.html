Cork Internet eXchange (CIX)

CIX is privately owned by EU nationals. CIX is located exclusively within Ireland, a country within the EU, and is subject only to Irish and EU law.

Latency: CIX is the EU data centre with the lowest latency to North America and the Irish data centre with the lowest latency to London. Direct subsea connectivity from Cork to France is anticipated in 2018 providing redundant, low latency, connectivity for data centre and cloud computing clients in France, and onward to countries such as The Netherlands, Germany, Spain and Portugal. Because CIX has low average latency to both Europe and North America, and it has good latency to the rest of the world, it is arguably the best single data centre in the world from which to serve digital assets. Over the medium term, Arctic Fibre has proposed a direct linkage from CIX to Asian markets, via the Arctic Circle, as well as a second ultra low latency route from CIX into London via Highbridge.

Resiliency: Low latency is supported by diverse routes for resiliency. Multiple data halls support rack level redundancy.

Competitive Pricing: CIX is extremely competitive. CIX is ideally located for European businesses seeking a base within the scope of Ireland's 12.5% tax regime.
